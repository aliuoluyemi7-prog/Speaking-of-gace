About

A thinker shaped in classrooms,
not seminaries.

Teacher. Christian writer. Builder of tools. A Yoruba man who found Christ without abandoning Orí.

The Teacher

Twenty years in front of a classroom

For over two decades, my days have started the same way — chalk, a scheme of work, and a room full of young people who need Economics, Government, and Social Studies to mean something beyond an exam. Teaching secondary school in Birnin Kebbi, Kebbi State, has taught me more about patience, clarity, and the discipline of explanation than any seminary could. It has also taught me that most students are not short on intelligence — they are short on tools built for their actual circumstances.

That conviction — that good tools should meet people where they are, not where a Silicon Valley product roadmap assumes they are — shapes everything else I build.

The Writer

Faith that thinks, and thought that believes

Speaking of Grace exists because I could not find the books I needed. Western systematic theology often arrives as an import, unaware of the questions an African Christian actually carries. Yoruba philosophy is rich but rarely brought into honest conversation with the gospel. I write at that intersection — grace, Yoruba thought, and the Western intellectual tradition held together rather than forced to choose.

My published work, including the Rabbit Hole series — sixteen volumes across three books (The Surface Breaks, Below the Waterline, The Deepest Room) — fuses neuroscience, Yoruba philosophy, Western intellectual tradition, and Christian theology into a single sustained argument: that faith and rigorous thought were never meant to be enemies.

The Builder

Technology as an act of stewardship

Every product I build — Chinexo, CashinSight, ExamWiseNG — starts from the same constraint I live inside: a smartphone, intermittent connectivity, and no formal IT background. If a tool works under those conditions, it will work for the schools, churches, and small businesses across Nigeria that share them. I build offline-first, mobile-first, and distribution-first, because a brilliant tool nobody can reach helps no one.

This is what it means to be an AI builder and educational technology creator from the classroom outward: not chasing novelty, but shipping software that a school proprietor in Kebbi State can actually adopt on Monday morning.

The Vision

One conviction, four expressions

"Does this create the moment when a reader thinks: someone finally said the thing I have been carrying alone?"

That single test governs every essay, book, and product under the Speaking of Grace name. The vision is simple: serve the thinking Christian — in the pew, in the classroom, and in the marketplace — with writing and tools that are honest enough to survive scrutiny and gracious enough to still be gospel.
